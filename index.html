Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among Chameleons
  • Most Chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

  2. Pegions are renowed for their outstanding navigation abilities
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Interesting Facts About Camels

  • Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.
  • Camels are specially adapted to the life in desert.
  • Camels eyes have three eyelids and two rows of eyelashes that prevent sand to enter their eyes.

Interesting Facts About Bears

  1. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals and care deeply about family members
  2. Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.
  3. Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim and can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • they eat other vegetation, fish, or small animals, but bamboo accounts for 99 percent of their diets.

Interesting Facts About Flying Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs most have red-orange eyes.
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  3. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

Interesting Facts About Puppies

  • Puppies are born blind and deaf.They spend 15–20 hours a day sleeping
  • Puppies become 'adults' when they turn one. They like when you sing-talk to them.
  • Puppies are born without teeth. The number of puppies depends on the breed

Interesting Facts About Cats

  1. Cats are believed to be the only mammals who don't taste sweetness.
  2. Cats are nearsighted, but their peripheral vision and night vision are much better than that of humans.
  3. Cats are supposed to have 18 toes (five toes on each front paw; four toes on each back paw). Cats can jump up to six times their length.

Interesting Facts About Pomeranian

  • The name of the breed comes from Pomerania, which is the region of Northern Europe where they originated.
  • They are also known affectionately as Poms or Pom Poms.Pomeranians weren't always so little.
  • These small dogs typically weigh around 7 to 10 pounds, but they're descended from big sled dogs.
